# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary visitors (today):** small companies and non-technical individuals — local-first (Mangaluru / India) — evaluating whether to hire AAF11 to build software or AI for them. They arrive not deeply technical, needing to quickly trust that this team can deliver and won't disappear after launch.

**Secondary visitors:** prospective partners / reference clients (e.g. WRNXT, GRID), collaborators, and future hires sizing up the team.

**The job to be done:** "Show me, fast, that AAF11 is a real, credible engineering company I can trust with my problem — what they've built, who's behind it, and why they're different from a generic shop." Decision-stage, low-patience, trust-driven.

## Product Purpose

The public site (`apps/web`) is AAF11's front door and primary credibility surface. It exists to convert curiosity into a conversation: communicate who AAF11 is, prove the track record, and make starting a project obvious. The home page is the conversion-critical fold — it must land the identity and the proof within ~5 seconds.

AAF11 is a three-founder product studio (AI systems + full-stack today; IoT/embedded next) born from hackathons, now building a sustainable, repeatable delivery company. Its sharpest edge is **applied AI in real domains** — especially healthcare/life-sciences, via an in-house biotech founder. Success for this site: a visitor understands the offer, believes the team, and reaches out.

## Brand Personality

**Three words: engineering-rooted, complete, unflinching.**

- **The name is the brand.** AAF11 is a hexadecimal signature, read letter-by-letter — *A · A · F · eleven*, never "aafeleven." The `11` is binary for three — the three founders. Attitude line: **"Built to eleven."** The identity is literally made by engineers; the design should encode that (hex, binary, signal, system) rather than decorate around it.
- **Voice:** precise, confident, technically honest. Shows the work rather than claiming it. No hype words, no agency fluff. An engineer you'd trust in an incident, not a salesperson.
- **Emotional goal:** trust through competence + a flash of distinctiveness. The visitor should feel "these people are the real thing" and remember the mark.
- **Complete team as a feeling:** business (Darel), domain/ops (Prateek), engineering (Rafan) — full from day one. Confidence without arrogance.
- **Fast, full-delivery:** hackathon-bred speed; ships the full extent of what it can build; gives every client something unique.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dark + purple/cyan gradient** — the current site's `#7c5cff`→`#22d3ee` is the saturated 2026 AI-startup tell. Drop it. (Identity colors are charcoal-black + orange, from the real logo.)
- **Templated agency look** — identical icon-heading-text card grids, stock "digital agency" hero, hero-metric template. AAF11 is not a faceless agency; don't look like one.
- **Eyebrow-on-every-section / 01·02·03 numbered scaffolding** — AI grammar. Use a deliberate cadence instead.
- **Monospace-as-costume** is the usual brand ban — but here mono is *earned*: the company name is hexadecimal and the team is an engineering team. Use it as identity, not decoration.
- Not cute, not playful-startup, not "revolutionary." Not editorial-magazine (display-serif + italic drop caps) — wrong register for an engineering studio.

## Design Principles

1. **The mark does the work.** Lead with the hex/binary identity (charcoal + orange, mono, "Built to eleven"). The name explains itself; let it.
2. **Show, don't tell.** Prove credibility with real shipped projects (ProtEngine, BUSLINK, Vigilens, RxBridge, Feedback Automation) and the real founding trio — not adjectives. The team photo and logo are first-class assets.
3. **Practice what you preach.** The site runs on Nexus and can surface live project status — the portfolio literally runs on the platform it's selling. Use that.
4. **Domain credibility is the moat.** Foreground the biotech/healthcare-AI edge; it's the thing a pure-software shop can't claim.
5. **Distinctive over safe.** Brand register: restraint without intent reads as mediocre. Commit to the identity; risk a little strangeness over looking like every other dev-studio template.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **Target: WCAG 2.1 AA.** Body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1, placeholders ≥4.5:1. Verify orange-on-charcoal and any muted text against this — orange on dark can fail at small sizes.
- Full keyboard navigation; visible focus states.
- `prefers-reduced-motion`: every animation needs a crossfade/instant alternative (the identity leans on motion/signal, so this matters).
- Don't rely on color alone for status/meaning (color-blind safe): pair with label/icon/shape.
