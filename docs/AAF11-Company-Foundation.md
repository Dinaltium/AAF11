## 1. The Name **[locked]**

**AAF11** is a hexadecimal signature, read **letter by letter — A · A · F · eleven** — never as a single word (not "aafeleven").

- **The mark:** AAF11 is written in hexadecimal — `A`, `A`, and `F` are all valid hexadecimal digits — and is read character by character — *A · A · F · eleven* — never blended into a word. This gives the name a deliberately technical, engineering-rooted identity. Because it carries no literal dictionary meaning, it stays domain-neutral: AAF11 can grow across software, AI, and hardware without ever outgrowing its own name.
- **The meaning:** the **`11`** is binary for **three** — a direct reference to the three founders who built the company. The name encodes the team itself.

## 2. Origin & Purpose — Why AAF11 Exists **[locked]**

AAF11 started as a team that entered hackathons and built small projects — three people with very different strengths who worked well together under pressure. After a run of strong results, the decision was simple: stop operating as a loose group of people completing tasks, and become a company.

That changed what the work is *for*. AAF11 moved from building for competitions to **solving real problems for clients** — delivering services and products that keep customers happy and turn one-off projects into long-term relationships. The difference between a group of developers and a company is **systems**: predictable delivery, documentation, reputation, and value that outlasts any single person. AAF11 exists to make that capability **sustainable and repeatable**, and to build lasting partnerships rather than just finish tasks.

## 7. Track Record **[settled — may expand]**

A record of what AAF11 has actually shipped, in roughly the order it was built.

**Feedback Automation** — AAF11's first product. Fully automates and dynamically handles student course-feedback submission, removing all manual effort. Also the team's first hands-on lesson in monetizing a tool and the realities of pricing for a student user base.

**Embed Club Website** — The website for the college's Embed (embedded systems) club. Largely complete; pending final testing and migration to the live domain to replace the old site.

**ProtEngine** — A drug-screening and simulation platform. It runs candidate drugs against mutated proteins (cancer and other mutation-driven diseases), ranks the most promising options, and generates research-style analysis of the results. Positioned deliberately as a **fast assistant tool**, not a replacement for the gold-standard tools researchers rely on: where those can take months, ProtEngine returns quick directional results to accelerate early-stage work. (Concept and backend/tooling led by Prateek; built for NITTE Hackfest.)

**dkbus / BUSLINK** — A bus-information platform for Mangaluru, giving riders route and stop information. In active development.

**Vigilens** — A disaster-misinformation detection tool. When fake, AI-generated disaster videos spread on social media, Vigilens detects whether a video is synthetic, inspects its metadata, and traces its origin via reverse search — then alerts the community (via Telegram) when content is fabricated.

**RxBridge** — A clinical decision-support tool. Where ProtEngine targets drug discovery, RxBridge focuses on the clinic: it uses Bayesian models to help doctors identify likely conditions and supports clearer doctor–patient communication. (Concept and domain led by Prateek.)

**WhatsApp Bot (Baileys)** — An automation and notification bot built on Baileys, used as an ambient messaging and notification layer.

**AAF11 Nexus** — The team's own internal operations platform, currently in build: a unified hub that ties AAF11's projects, data, and notifications together. The first product AAF11 is building for itself.
