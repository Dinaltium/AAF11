# AAF11 — Growth & Plan

*Working document · v1 · internal reference*

How AAF11 grows: who it serves now and next, how the services expand, and what the company is working toward. Companion document: **AAF11 — Company Foundation** (who AAF11 is).

---

## 1. Where We Are Now

AAF11 has the capability and the track record; the next job is turning that into steady, paying work.

- **Capability:** AI systems and full-stack development, proven across a portfolio of real and competition projects.
- **Team:** three founders covering business (Darel), domain/operations (Prateek), and engineering (Rafan).
- **Momentum:** early industry conversations underway (e.g. WRNXT, GRID), and the company's own foundations in build — the AAF11 website (`aaf11.tech`) and the AAF11 Nexus internal hub.
- **Stage:** moving from project-and-hackathon work toward a predictable client base. Systems, pipeline, and reputation are the gaps to close.

---

## 2. Who We Serve — and Who We'll Serve Next

AAF11 doesn't have a deep, fixed customer plan yet, and that's intentional at this stage — the focus is on serving clients what they actually need while learning the market.

- **Now:** small companies and individuals who aren't deeply technical, helping them leverage AI and software to grow. Local first (Mangaluru / India).
- **Next:** expand to different locations across India as delivery and reputation build.
- **Future:** international clients and broader markets.

The through-line: start by helping smaller players who don't know much about tech get real leverage from it, then widen the circle as AAF11 proves it can deliver at each step.

---

## 3. Services Roadmap

Services expand only as the team can deliver each one **fully and reliably** — capability first, then offer.

| Horizon | Services | Notes |

## 4. Growth Phases

**Phase 1 — Build (now, ~0–12 months)**
- Deliver for small-to-medium clients and build a real track record.
- Ship current commitments: BUSLINK, Embed Club site, the AAF11 website, and Nexus.
- Put core systems in place: delivery process, documentation, client communication, simple client tracking.
- Start consistent client outreach — build a pipeline rather than waiting for opportunities.

**Phase 2 — Establish (~1–3 years)**
- A predictable client pipeline and the first recurring clients.
- Turn delivered work into case studies and a portfolio that sells confidence.
- Add the IoT & embedded service line.
- Expand reach across India.
- Formalize AAF11's long-term vision, earned from a proven delivery record.

**Phase 3 — Expand (~3 years+)**
- National and international reach; larger clients and partnerships.
- AAF11 known as a company that solves business problems through technology.

---

## 5. How We'll Grow

The operating principles behind the phases above:

- **Reputation is the engine.** Deliver fully and reliably; satisfied clients and case studies drive the next clients.
- **Build a pipeline, don't wait.** Consistent outreach, follow-ups, and partnership-building every week.
- **Get faster as you scale.** Reusable systems and components so quality holds and delivery speeds up as projects increase.
- **Document everything.** Every delivered project becomes a case study and a portfolio asset.
- **Keep experimenting.** Bring each client something unique — that's the differentiator, not the lowest price.
